My Experience

Under construction!

During the summer of 2016, I worked in a local branch of Thermo Fisher Scientific as a Software Engineering Intern. There, I speed-learned the basics of web development using the Django framework. I was able to refresh my knowledge of HTML/CSS and Python while learning JQuery, git, terminal, and Bootstrap. I used my newly-acquired skills to develop a CRUD app that allows users to enter, edit, and delete experiment data from a sortable table. Each experiment entry would then automatically link to the list of run results based on the experiment ID. This table included a dropdown list of columns so users can hide certain columns if they desire.

I was also the Co-Founder and former President of my school's Computer Science Club. While I was in charge of the club, I organized trips, guest speakers, and a light hackathon. The two trips I organized were a trip to New Jersey for the HackRU hackathon, and a trip to New York for a tour of the Spotify offices. The guest speakers included people with startups and startup ideas, a man from NewHaven.IO, and a Senior Technical Evangelist from Microsoft. The light hackathon, known as FemHack, was an 8-hour hackathon exclusively for girls in grade school. This was part of the YouthZone component to the biennial Women's Studies Conference, hosted by my university's Women's Studies department.
